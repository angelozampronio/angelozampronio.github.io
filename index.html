Angelo Zampronio

I hold a degree in Computational Physics from the University of São Paulo (USP) and am currently pursuing a Master’s degree as a CAPES fellow. My academic background provides a strong foundation in mathematics, statistics, and scientific programming, and throughout my training I have developed a deep interest in data analysis, statistical modeling, and the study of complex physical systems. My Master’s research focuses on the development of a machine learning–based noise spectroscopy method applied to the simulation of quantum logic gates. The project involves mathematical modeling, computational simulations, exploratory data analysis, data cleaning and organization, feature engineering, time-series analysis, and the implementation and validation of machine learning models. The ultimate goal is to infer noise distributions from quantum measurement data, extracting relevant information from noisy and complex datasets.
